What is Cyber Crime Insurance ?

Cyber insurance (cyber security insurance) is a type of insurance that facilitates businesses to reduce any financial risks against malware and ransomware cyber-attacks. The cyber crime insurance policy acts as a safety net and supports businesses with financial losses when attacked by cybercriminals causing fraudulent transactions, unauthorized access to confidential data leading to cyber extortion, etc. when business reputation and operational continuity are at risk.

Who is Cyber Security Insurance for ?

The cyber-attacks can have a devastating impact on the company finances inducing substantial losses and operational blockers. Without a good cyber insurance policy coverage, many companies will have an extensive struggle to fight against the threats and compensate for the financial and reputational damage.

The key sectors which are highly prone to cyber-attacks are:

  • Information and Technology

  • BFSI & Fintech

  • Healthcare

  • Manufacturing

  • Finance

  • Energy

  • Consumer Services

  • Government

  • Retail and E-commerce

With comprehensive cybersecurity measures, which primarily include cyber insurance, companies can safeguard their businesses easily. This ensures financial protection against the impact of cyber-attacks, releasing their bandwidth to focus on product innovation and business growth without any worries about potential cyber incidents.

What's included in Cyber Insurance Policy Coverage?

Cyber insurance policy coverage provides an array of extensive services to normalize the disruption caused by cybercriminals and resume business operations at the earliest. The cyber insurance covers for the following aspects:

  • Malware Attack: When a malware attack harms the computerâ€™s business systems, the cyber insurance coverage provides resources for recovery of the affected systems and defense costs for any legal claims.

  • Phishing: Cover for any financial losses incurred due to unauthorized access to any confidential records and financial frauds induced by it.

  • Identity Theft: The cyber security insurance policy offers protection against any incidental identity theft and supports the expenses for any legal proceedings and any potential settlements after the verdict.

  • Data Breach or Privacy: Cover for legal costs when the business is impacted by a data breach or invasion of data privacy.

  • Reputational Damage: Comprehensive coverage for any financial losses due to reputational damage led by a specific cyber incident, which also extends to the loss in profits and expenses for public relations campaigns and crisis management activities varying according to the policy premium.

  • Cyber Extortion: Cover for any ransomware attacks when cybercriminals demand payment to return access to data or systems. The cyber insurance policy coverage could provide financial compensation for ransom payments, support negotiation with cyber criminals and facilitate a quicker recovery.

  • Computer Forensics: Financial support for any expenses involved in the investigation of a cyberattack, including employing a forensic expert to assess the affected systems, retrieve the data, and detect any potential gaps. Insurers help the insured get clarity on the data breach and enforce security measures to prevent future attacks.

  • Extended Reporting Period: The cyber crime insurance coverage offers an extended period for businesses to report any incidents or attacks that occurred after the policy expired with an additional premium. This ensures auxiliary protection for any claims arising from past incidents and continuity of coverage.

What's not Covered under Cyber Insurance?

  • Any claims from pre-existing data privacy breaches or other cyber incidents like IT theft loss, cyber extortion, cyberstalking, malware damage, etc. before the purchase of the policy is not included in the cyber insurance policy coverage.

  • The overall expenditure to enhance the technology systems and strengthen the current safety systems of the businesses.

  • When any employee or insider has initiated the cyberattack, the insured will not get the benefit of coverage from the insurer.

  • Cyber insurance excludes any claims about a physical injury, sickness, disability, or death. However, the exception is any case of proven mental anguish due to the cyber attack may be covered.

  • When the insured or partners are intentionally non-compliant or have caused breaches or fraudulent activities, the cyber crime insurance does not cover it.

  • The claims due to warlike situations like an invasion, activities of foreign enemies, rebellion or revolutionary acts, or government actions are excluded from the coverage.

What does a Cyber Risk Insurance cost?

The cyber insurance policy premium cost for businesses can be influenced by a number of factors. This includes the nature of the business, the coverage desired with any additional sub-limits, the size of the organization, the risk potential, current cybersecurity measures, previous record of claims and history of cyberattacks. Generally, the annual cyber insurance policy premium for businesses begins from a few lakhs and can vary according to desired coverage limit and other factors.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a cyber insurance policy cover?

A cyber insurance policy coverage includes any event of cyberattacks and data breaches. The policy will provide cover for cyber extortion, data breaches, liability costs, legal representation, crisis management, reputational damage, and forensic investigation.

Is cyber security insurance worth it?

Yes, cyber insurance is worth buying given the surge in cybercrimes with the dynamic penetration of AI and technology into businesses. A comprehensive cyber insurance policy offers the protection against any financial losses and reputational damage in the event of a cyber-attack or data breach which caused operational disruptions and legal liabilities. The cyber security insurance is a shield to any digital discrepancies.

How to choose the best Cyber Crime Insurance Policy?

When selecting the best cyber insurance policy for your business, some of the key parameters you must consider are:

  • Third party liability which includes legal defense and verdict settlement

  • Direct losses endured by the policyholder due to cyber crime with data recovery and losses due lack of business continuity.

  • Legal expenses along with data breaches.

  • Reputational damage and PR crisis.

  • Identity theft protection.
