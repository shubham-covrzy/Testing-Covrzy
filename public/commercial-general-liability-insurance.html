What is Commercial General Liability Insurance?

Commercial general liability (also known as General Commercial Insurance) is an insurance policy which offers coverage for any damages or losses caused to third person or property due to operations or activities within the business premises which is insured. This includes third party product and public liability.

Commercial general liability offers a compensatory amount for any physical injury to third party, property damage and legal expenses incurred in resolving claims due to damage or losses induced by on-going business activities

Why should you buy Commercial General Liability Policy?

Generally, all businesses are prone to liability risks and have a probability to cause un-intentional harm. Commercial general insurance is imperative for certain industries like food and beverages, cosmetics and other beauty products, pharma, real estate companies, and manufacturing sectors where there are high chances of injury or damage. Further, service-based businesses, such as transport agencies, construction companies, and healthcare services also have liability risks. With a CGL insurance policy, businesses get the required financial security with cover for legal costs, and verdict settlement for lawsuits initiated by impacted third parties.

The businesses with CGL policy demonstrate strong commitment and preparedness for unprecedented risks. This is not only efficient risk management but also builds credibility of the company. Furthermore, with the rising claims on injury or loss caused within the insured property premises, it might impact the reputation of the company and also cause personal injury like slander or defamation. CGL policy ensures businesses have a professional reputation and minimize liability claims. Several organizations may have a mandatory clause to only work with businesses having commercial general insurance, making it important for contractual obligations. With a CGL policy, businesses can safeguard from the heavy compensatory amounts, protect the company assets, and prevent any reputational damage due to third-party claims causing crisis situations.

What’s included in Commercial General Liability Coverage?

  • Physical injury: For any injury to a third-party due to unintentional activities amid business operations or product utility, CGL policy provides the required compensation for medical expenses and supports the injured on behalf of the insured.

  • Property damage: When business operations cause property damage owned by a third party, the commercial general liability insurance provides compensation to recover and repair the damages caused and restore the property to its earlier condition.

  • Advertising injury or IP infringement claims: Coverage for any claims arising due to the advertising activities of the business, which extends to copyright infringement or allegations of promoting any false or exaggerated information about the product. However, different insurance companies have varying coverage limits according to the policy and business requirements.

  • Legal defense costs: The commercial general liability insurance covers legal expenses for lawsuits due to claims arising from third parties for damage or losses due to business operations. The coverage provides compensation to manage the attorney fees, and any settlement costs after judgment.

What’s not Covered under Commercial General Liability Insurance Policy?

  • Intentional wrongdoing: The policy excludes coverage for any damages caused intentionally during the business operations or for any damage caused by the employees within the insured premises.

  • Contractual liabilities: This policy is not responsible for any coverage in case of any obligations assumed under contracts which surpass the coverage guidelines for liability.

  • Injury caused to employees: This policy does not cover any medical expenses or legal claims for any injury caused to employees during business operations or product utility. However, the same will be covered in Workers’ Compensation insurance.

  • Professional negligence: For any errors, omissions or negligence caused when providing professional service, the commercial general liability insurance does not provide any coverage to represent the business for such claims.

  • Personal property or assets: The commercial general liability insurance is intended to insure any damage or losses caused to third-party and will exclude any coverage for damage caused to the insured property contents or personal assets of the insured.

  • Data breach: The data leaks, or breach of confidential data is not a part of the commercial general liability coverage, and will have a distinct insurance policy.

  • Product guarantee and maintenance: For any issues such as product defects, wear and tear and any claims about the functioning of the products, there are distinct policies, and the CGL insurance does not provide any compensation for the same.

How does Commercial General Liability Insurance work?

General Commercial Insurance is a type of liability insurance policy that provides coverage for any physical injury to a third party or property damage or any claims arising from it. When any unprecedented event occurs inducing insured to raise a claim under commercial general liability policy, the most immediate task should be to update the insurance company about the accident. Further, aggregate all the documents together with evidence of the damage (photographs, records of witnesses present and any official documents). When all the documents and forms related to initiating claims are submitted, the insurer will conduct an investigation to assess the damages and required compensatory amount. After the review, the insurer makes the final call to accept or reject the claims, and the coverage amount in case the claims for general commercial insurance are valid and eligible for settlement.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is CGL Insurance?

CGL insurance, also known as Commercial General Liability insurance, is an insurance policy that supports businesses in any third-party risks led by business activities and operations such as physical injury to third-party, property damage and any legal claims over products or injuries within the insured premises of business.

What are the Documents Required for Comprehensive General Liability Insurance Claim?

To raise a claim under general liability insurance, the following documents are needed:

  • Policy documents with sum insured and details about risk potential related to the nature of business

  • Documents related to company premises and GST filing.

  • Financial records: The insurer needs more clarity on revenue and business capacity which is easy to evaluate with the financial records like account statements and profit or loss entry.

  • Prior history of claims: The insurance company needs a history of claims to evaluate coverage limit and premium prices based on the record of risk factors.

  • Contracts and Agreements: The insured should provide a copy of authorized ownership or contracts with liability clauses.

  • Business permits and entity documents: The insurer might require all business-related documents and proof of ownership which includes documentation like partnership agreements, record of transactions, approval forms and operational licenses.

How much does Commercial General Liability Insurance Cost?

The cost of premium for commercial general liability insurance is evaluated considering several factors tailored according to the specific business requirements. This includes the size of the company, the nature of industry, the location of the business premises, and the amount of coverage required as per the risk potential of the business.

Who needs General Commercial Insurance?

Commercial General Liability insurance is vital for all businesses due to high potential for liability risks. This policy is ideal for retail businesses from boutiques to supermarkets, service - based businesses like construction companies, transport agencies, healthcare services, restaurants and cafes (FnB), manufacturing factories, and startups.
