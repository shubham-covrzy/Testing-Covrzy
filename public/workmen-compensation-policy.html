Whatâ€™s not Covered under Workmen Compensation Insurance Policy ?

Workmen Compensation insurance provides compensation for employees injured onsite. However, there are some conditions when the policy does not provide required coverage.

  • War, riots or invasion: When any employee is injured on the working site as a result of war, invasion, military or rebel activities, nuclear weapon materials, radioactive contamination via nuclear fuel or nuclear waste, the WC policy does not provide any coverage to support the injured employees.

  • Accidents beyond commercial premises: In the event of any accidents causing injury to employees in any commercial premises which are not enlisted in the policy schedule, there will be no coverage for medical expenses or income loss of injured employees.

  • Self-injury or deliberate negligence: The WC policy does not provide any coverage for cases when the employee is injured or unfit for employment due to self-harm or sheer negligence to perform risky tasks when already injured due to external factors.

  • Initial days of disability: The policy does not offer any compensation for the first 3 days of total disablement in the event the employee experiences disablement for a period of less than 28 days.

  • Non-business injuries: For any injuries caused to the employee outside the company premises or beyond the scope of employment duties, the policy will not provide any coverage for injury or loss of life.

  • Drug or Alcohol consumption: When the employee is not severely injured and was working under the influence of drugs or alcohol, the WC policy does not provide any coverage for medical expenses or income loss.
