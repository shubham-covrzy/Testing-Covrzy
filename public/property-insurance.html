What is Property Insurance?

Property Insurance is a type of insurance policy that provides coverage for property owned by businesses in the event of any damages by natural or man-made disasters. This can include buildings, equipment, inventory, and other property that is essential to the operation of the business. The property insurance policy provides coverage for any losses or damage caused due to perils like fire, theft, or natural disasters and supports the businesses to cover the costs of repair or replacement.

Why should you Purchase Property Insurance ?

Typically, the property insurance policy is designed for businesses of all sizes that own or lease commercial property. This can include retail stores, offices, manufacturing facilities, warehouses, and other commercial properties. Business owners who have invested a significant amount of money in their physical assets need property insurance coverage to protect their property from unexpected events like property damage.

Without insurance coverage, businesses may face significant costs to repair or replace damaged property, which can lead to financial instability or even bankruptcy. The property insurance policy can provide peace of mind to business owners, knowing that their property is protected. The property insurance coverage also protects against any legal claims if any injury is caused to a third party within the property premises. The property insurance in India acts as a security for lenders when seeking a mortgage as it ensures their investment is protected.

Features of Covrzy’s Property Insurance ?

Let’s explore the different features of property insurance policy as mentioned here -

  • Comprehensive policy coverage: The property insurance coverage includes protection for both the structure and the contents of the property, providing a safety net for the property owners, from entrepreneurs to shopkeepers.

  • Coverage for property contents: The policy will also provide coverage when the fittings and fixtures are damaged.

What’s included in Property Insurance Coverage

The property insurance policy offers coverage for any damage or losses caused to the property in the event of a fire, vandalism, robbery, and certain natural disasters according to the policy. Let’s understand the inclusions of property insurance here –

  • Physical Structures: The property insurance policy offers protection for any losses or damages on the property structure induced by
    • Man-made hazards like theft, vandalism, fire
    • Natural disasters storms, floods, earthquakes, cyclones, etc.
    • Acts of Terrorism

  • Contents and belongings on Property: In the event of damage caused to the property, property insurance offers coverage for financial losses as a result of damage caused to contents as well as personal belongings on property such as furniture, artifacts, electronic gadgets, etc.

  • Additional operational expenses: When the property is heavily damaged, the property insurance policy will provide coverage for temporary office premises to ensure business continuity.

What’s not Covered under Property Insurance?

Property insurance provides coverage for any financial losses to the property, but there are a few conditions when a property insurance policy becomes redundant. Here are the policy exclusions for property insurance coverage:

  • Negligence or Intentional damage: When the property is not maintained and the damage is induced by lack of maintenance or an intentional act of the property owner, the property insurance will not provide any coverage.

  • Cost of the property: The property insurance does not cover the cost of the property/land in certain conditions which vary according to the different insurers.

  • War: Damage invoked by events such as war, attack by a foreign enemy, and invasion are not covered under property insurance. Some insurers may provide coverage with an add-on premium.

  • Property maintenance: The property insurance coverage does not include the cost of maintenance and renovation to manage the usual wear and tear.

  • Specific personal assets: Some specific assets of the owner may not be directly covered under property insurance and need an add-on cover based on the value of the items.

How does Property Insurance work?

Property insurance protects against any damages induced by natural disasters and man-made accidents to buildings, factories, shops, and offices along with personal assets. The policy coverage also includes outbuildings such as garages, parking spaces, pools, fences, etc. to ensure additional security. Furthermore, the policy also assures legal liability and medical expenditure on any damage to third parties within property premises.

When initiating a claim for property insurance, compile the required standard documents which include the complete policy document with duration of coverage, evidence of physical damage to the property (photographs, receipts), required certificates related to the property, a copy of the FIR, claims form, and any other policy documents.

The insurer will initiate an investigation of the property and review the documentation. The claims settlement amount will be decided after the investigation and the funds will be deposited into the insured’s bank account details given while purchasing the property insurance online or offline. The insurer will verify your earlier claims and history of premium payments before releasing the claims funds.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Property Insurance Cost?

The property insurance cost significantly depends on the location of the property, the type of property insurance purchased, the cost per square foot, the condition and maintenance of the property, and the value of the property contents. Generally, the premium of property insurance in India starts from around ₹5000.

Which Property Insurance should an SME buy?

For good financial protection of their physical assets, Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) should select a comprehensive commercial property insurance policy. This property insurance coverage includes their equipment, structure (building), inventory, and liability risks that could otherwise cause interference in the business operations.

How is Property Insurance Calculated?

The factors influencing the property insurance cost are the location of the property, the value of the property, the types of property insurance purchased, the price per square foot of the property, the value of the contents on the property, etc. A comprehensive premium is calculated and updated on specific calculators featured on the insurer’s website when you want to buy property insurance online. The calculators can give precise property insurance cost and coverage limits in 3 steps –
  • Select your insurance requirements – structure and/or content
  • Fill all the details related to your property
  • Select the coverage and any add-ons to make the comprehensive cover
After completing the last step, the calculator will reflect the premium of the property insurance coverage.

What is the difference between Property Insurance and Home Insurance?

The property insurance is a broader category that provides comprehensive cover for all physical assets like commercial property like shops, factories, and corporate buildings, and residential property that includes apartments or individual homes. The home insurance is a very specific category of property insurance and might have additional specifications in the coverage and exclusions of the property insurance policy.
