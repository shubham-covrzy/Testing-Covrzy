What is Fire Insurance?

Fire insurance is a specific insurance policy which provides protection against any financial losses and structural damages in the event of fire-related accidents. The fire insurance coverage includes damage caused to the buildings, property assets, inventory and equipment any other structural damage in the event of fire, explosion, smoke, etc. The policy provides financial security for any unexpected fire hazards.

Why do you need a Fire and Burglary Insurance?

Fire accidents can strike unexpectedly creating havoc on the business with damage to the property structure and personal assets. With a robust fire insurance policy, the business owners would have a safety net to prevent heavy financial losses with adequate coverage. In the event of a fire accident, there could be severe damage caused to property structure, property contents which includes valuables, inventory, equipment, cash deposit, etc. The fire insurance coverage ensures the recovery expenses for property and assets alongside business continuity are taken care of. Fire insurance is vital for businesses, especially SMEs, hotels, factories and manufacturing units, shops and retail stores and commercial buildings in the event of any unfortunate fire accident.

What’s included in Fire Insurance Coverage ?

  • Damage caused to property structure: Fire insurance coverage includes damage caused directly by fire, explosion, and missile testing operations that damage property. Further, the fire insurance covers any loss or damage from an aircraft or its parts falling onto the insured property, and damage by movement of earth or rock across a slope.

  • Contents coverage: Alongside the structure of the property, the fire insurance also safeguards property belongings, business inventory, equipment or machinery, personal assets like electronic gadgets, cash, documents, jewellery, etc.

  • Liability protection: Fire insurance provides liability coverage that offers protection to the policyholder in the event of a fire accident spreading from the insured property to neighbouring properties.

  • Add-on cover for additional expenses : In the event the fire accident has damaged the property and made it unfit for further business till recovered, the fire insurance coverage provides required compensation for temporary accommodation.

What’s not Covered under Fire and Burglary Insurance ?

  • Willful Misconduct: Losses caused intentionally by the insured are not included in the fire insurance coverage.

  • Missing Property: The fire insurance policy does not provide any cover when personal assets or property contents disappear without evidence of fire accident.

  • Temperature Change in Cold Storage: The stock spoilage due to temperature fluctuations in cold storage is excluded in fire insurance unless the temperature change is a result of fire accident.

  • Prior & Pending Claims: An existing or ongoing claim related to fire damage before inception of the policy is not included in fire insurance.

  • War, invasion, or civil unrest: Damage from war, invasion, or civil unrest is typically excluded.

How does Fire and Burglary Insurance work?

In the event of a fire accident, the insured can initiate the claims process for fire insurance by updating the insurer and police authorities. Further, the insured should aggregate evidence iterating the damage caused to the property structure and personal assets through photographs, videos and list of property contents. Then, the insured can fill the claims form, submit the required documents. The insurer will assess the claim and damage caused by fire accident by appointing a surveyor and estimate the final settlement amount to compensate for the damage caused to the personal assets and property structure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Burglary Insurance?

Burglary insurance helps protect the damages caused to the commercial or residential property in the event of an unlawful, forced or violent entry with the intention of theft, vandalism, or assault and cause significant damage to the property structure and contents which may include precious valuables. Burglary insurance ensures financial protection for stolen and damaged property contents and structural damage due to such events.

What are the types of fire Insurance?

There are different kinds of fire insurance varying according to the scope of coverage and the diverse business requirements. Some of the types of fire insurance are as follows:

  • Consequential Loss (Fire) Insurance: To secure companies from financial losses induced by business interruptions due to fire and recovery of any lost profits.

  • Valued Policy: The insurer assigns an estimate for the items or property at the time of policy inception of this fire insurance.

  • Specific Policy: This type of fire insurance provides a fixed compensation amount.

  • Floating Policy: This policy helps insure more than one property even if located in different cities.

How do you file Fire Insurance Claim?

When you have a fire accident at the property, the primary action should be to immediately update the insurer and inform the police authorities when applicable. The next step to claim fire insurance is to document the evidence of damage through photographs, videos and list of damaged property contents, complete the claims form and present supporting documents which includes reports from police, fire department and other associated records to streamline claims settlement for fire insurance policy. The insurance company conducts an assessment to estimate the damages caused to the property and its contents and then process the claim based on the assessment.

What are necessary Fire Insurance Policy Conditions?

The necessary fire insurance policy conditions are elicited as follows:

  • Disclosure of facts: The policyholder must provide precise details about the property which includes the value at the time of policy inception.

  • Premium payments: There should be timely payment of policy premium to ensure the fire insurance policy is active.

  • Property maintenance: The policyholder should maintain the property and follow preventive actions to reduce the risks.

  • Regulatory compliance: For recurrent validity of fire insurance coverage, it is important to abide by the standard safety regulations.

  • Notify any updates: The insured must regularly update the insurer for any changes in property use or value.

What is the principle of fire insurance?

The 5 core principles of fire insurance are as follows:

  • Principle of Indemnity: Restore the damages to earlier condition before loss.

  • Principle of Insurable Interest: The policyholder must have financial share in the property and should be impacted by the loss or damage.

  • Principle of utmost good faith: All the involved parties must be transparent and communicate all the relevant material facts associated to the property.

  • Principle of Proximate cause: This determines the core cause of damage and if it is covered, the claims process will be streamlined.

  • Principle of Subrogation: The insurers compensate the recovery costs from a third party causing the damage after claims settlement with the policyholder.
